# Vendor 3 Analysis: Customer Acquisition Strategy

## Problem Statement
Vendors are skeptical about whether there will be enough customers (consumers) on the platform to justify their time and effort.

## Proposed Strategy (Non-Software)
- **Contractor Network Integration**: Leverage the Admin's personal background as a contractor to bring in early-adopter professional buyers.
- **Targeted Outreach**: Directly contact contractors and builders who need consistent material supplies.
- **B2B Partnership**: Market the software as a tool for contractors to manage their projects, which naturally brings order volume to the vendors.

## How to Explain to Vendors
"I am a contractor myself. I already have the buyers ready. I’m not just building a website; I’m building a bridge between the contractors I work with and reliable vendors like you."

## Pros and Cons Analysis

| Stakeholder | Pros (Advantages) | Cons (Disadvantages) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Customer** | Access to a wide network of verified vendors through a trusted contractor platform. | None. |
| **Vendor** | Guaranteed order volume from professional buyers rather than just random retail shoppers. | High expectation of quality and timely delivery from professional contractors. |
| **Admin** | Organic growth; lower marketing costs compared to public advertising. | High reliance on the Admin's personal network for early success. |
