# Vendor 2 Analysis: Price Privacy & Competitive Spying

## Problem Statement
Vendors are afraid that competitors will create fake "Customer" accounts just to spy on their prices, leading to price wars or market crises.

## Proposed Changes
- **MRP Display Mode**: Allow vendors to set the "Market MRP" on the platform for public viewing.
- **"Request Individual/Bulk Price"**: Replace the fixed price for high-volume items with a button that starts a private chat or request between the customer and vendor.
- **Private Quoting**: The real "transactional" price is only revealed privately to the customer after the vendor reviews their profile.

## How to Explain to Vendors
"Your competitive edge is safe. Think of the website as your shop window—customers see the MRP, but the special 'dealer price' you give to a loyal contractor stays private between you and them."

## Pros and Cons Analysis

| Stakeholder | Pros (Advantages) | Cons (Disadvantages) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Customer** | Gets accurate MRP references; feels they are getting a 'special deal' during private negotiation. | Friction—cannot see the final price instantly for quick shopping. |
| **Vendor** | Total privacy from competitors; prevents price wars; allows for custom pricing based on customer relationship. | More manual work to respond to individual price requests. |
| **Admin** | Keeps vendors on the platform who would otherwise leave due to privacy fears. | Harder to automate checkout if every price needs a "request." |
